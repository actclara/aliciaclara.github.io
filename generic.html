Bike Sharing Customer Analysis

Increasing Annual Membership Riders in Chicago

Home

Background Information

Cyclistic is a fictional bike-sharing company based in Chicago. The marketing director believes the company's future depends on maximizing number of annual memberships. Additionally, financial analysts at the company have concluded that annual membership riders are much more profitable than casual riders.

Cyclistic has three types of pricing plans:

1. single-ride passes

2. full-day passes

3. annual memberships

Customers who purchase single-ride or full-day passes are considered as casual riders and customers who purchased annual memberships are called annual membership riders.

Objective

Understand differences between casual riders and annual membership riders, why casual riders would buy a membership, and how digital media can affect marketing tactics to influence casual riders to become annual membership riders.

About the Data

Used Cyclistic historical data to analyze and identify trends. The datasets have a different name because Cyclistic is a fictional company. The dataset has been made available publicly by Motivate International Inc. under license agreement. To protect data privacy, customer's personal information is undisclosed. At the making of this project, datasets considered are from July 2020 to June 2021 being the recent datasets available.
